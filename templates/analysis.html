Findings:
Across all provinces, the three highest cancer prevalences found were breast, prostate, and colorectal cancer. Ontario appears to have the highest number of most cancers, which could be due to Ontario having the highest population out of all provinces. Nunavut has the lowest number of most cancers which could also be attributed to its low population.
Sex differences:
Cancers more prevalent in females: breast, uterine, lung and bronchus
Cancers more prevalent in males: prostate, colorectal, bladder

Analysis:
The findings from the data suggest that there is a high prevalence of breast, prostate, and colorectal cancer across all provinces/territories, according to the Canadian Cancer Society dataset. However, the dataset dates back to latest 2017, which means true prevalence may have changed in recent times. With these findings however, our team proposes to acquire funding in clinical trials and research in the more prevalent cancers, in order to invent medicines and take more preventive measures.
In terms of implications, the map provides a visual representation of the areas with the highest number of individuals affected by the different types of cancers. This can help provincial government jurisdictions to plan resource allocation for healthcare in order to reduce the burden caused by cancer.
